10. Israelis, like United States, wary about strike on Iran

JERUSALEM — As Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu returned from a visit to Washington this week, much of the discussion here centered on traffic-light hues. Had the Israeli leader been given a red light or a green light about attacking Iran’s nuclear facilities? The general assessment was that President Obama’s signal — his appeals to give diplomacy more time and pledges of support for Israel — fell somewhere on the spectrum between yellow and red. To many here, that probably came as a relief.
